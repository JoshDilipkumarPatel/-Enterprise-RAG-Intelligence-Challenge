GDPR Compliance Readiness Assessment

Assessment Date: May 2026
Classification: Confidential

The compliance team completed the annual GDPR readiness assessment across all business units. The assessment evaluated data processing activities, consent management, data subject rights handling, and cross-border transfer mechanisms.

Key Findings:

1. Data Mapping Gaps: Three business units have incomplete records of processing activities under Article 30. The customer analytics team processes behavioral data without an updated Data Protection Impact Assessment.

2. Consent Management: The marketing platform collects email consent but does not record the specific processing purposes at the time of consent. This creates a risk of non-compliant processing under Article 7.

3. Data Subject Access Requests: The average response time for DSARs increased from 12 days to 19 days in Q1. The regulatory deadline is 30 days, but the trend requires corrective action before it becomes a compliance breach.

4. Cross-Border Transfers: Following the adequacy decision review, two vendor contracts require updated Standard Contractual Clauses. The legal team has initiated renegotiation with Fabrikam Cloud and Northwind Security.

5. Data Retention: The HR system retains former employee records for 7 years, which exceeds the 5-year retention policy approved by the Data Protection Officer. An automated purge job is scheduled for Q3.

Remediation Timeline:
- Article 30 records update: complete by June 30
- Consent mechanism redesign: complete by July 15
- DSAR process automation: procurement approved, deployment by August 1
- SCC renegotiation: target completion September 1
- HR data purge: scheduled for July 20

Risk Rating: Medium
Next Review: November 2026
